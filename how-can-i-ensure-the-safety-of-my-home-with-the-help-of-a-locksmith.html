Types of Locks: Know Your Options

When it comes to securing your home or business, locks play an important role. However, with so many types of locks available, it can be overwhelming to choose the right one. Knowing your options can help you make an informed decision when it comes to locking up your property.

Deadbolts: Deadbolts are a popular choice for securing exterior doors. They feature a locking mechanism that can only be opened with a key or a knob on the inside. Deadbolts come in single, double, and lockable thumb-turn varieties.

Knob Locks: Knob locks are commonly found on interior doors, but they can also be used on exterior doors in combination with another lock type. They feature a knob on one side and a lock on the other, but they can be easily bypassed by twisting the knob off.

Lever Handle Locks: Lever handle locks are easier to operate than knob locks and are ADA compliant. They are often found on interior doors but can also be used on exterior doors and come in various styles and finishes.

Smart Locks: Smart locks are gaining popularity due to their convenience and security features. They can be controlled via a smartphone app, keypad, or keycard. They also allow for temporary access codes for guests and can send alerts to the homeowner if the lock is tampered with.

Cylinder Locks: Cylinder locks are commonly found on filing cabinets, mailboxes, and sliding doors. They feature a cylindrical locking mechanism that requires a key to unlock.

Padlocks: Padlocks come in various shapes and sizes and are a versatile locking option. They can be used to secure lockers, gates, and storage units, and they can feature different locking mechanisms such as combination, key, and smart locks.

Now that you know about the different types of locks available, you can make an informed decision when it comes to securing your property. Remember, it's important to consult with a professional locksmith to ensure proper installation and maintenance of your chosen locks.

Insider Tips for Home Security

When it comes to securing your home, there are a few insider tips that can make a big difference in deterring burglars and keeping your family safe. Here are some expert tips for home security:

1. Use a deadbolt lock.

A deadbolt lock is an essential component of home security. It's important to use a high-quality deadbolt lock on all exterior doors, as it provides extra protection against forced entry. Make sure your deadbolt meets ANSI grade 1 standards, which means it has been tested for strength and durability.

2. Install a security system.

A security system is an effective way to keep your home safe. Choose a system with monitoring capabilities, so that if an intruder does attempt to enter your home, the authorities will be alerted. Make sure your security system includes motion detectors, door and window sensors, and a high-decibel alarm.

3. Keep your landscaping in check.

Tall shrubs and overgrown trees can provide cover for burglars, so it's important to keep your landscaping in check. Trim back any trees or shrubs that are close to your house, particularly near windows and doors. This will make it more difficult for burglars to gain access to your home without being seen.

4. Be mindful of social media.

Be careful how much information you share on social media. Posting that you're on vacation can alert burglars that your home is unoccupied. Avoid posting photos of expensive items, such as jewelry or electronics, as this can also make your home a target for theft.

5. Keep valuables out of sight.

Don't leave your valuables out in plain sight for all to see. Keep expensive items, such as jewelry and electronics, in a safe or hidden in a secure location. This will reduce the likelihood of theft, as burglars are less likely to target homes where they can't see any valuable items from the outside.

By following these insider tips for home security, you can help protect your home and keep your family safe. Remember, it's always better to be safe than sorry!

Frequently Asked Questions: Locksmithing and Home Security

Locksmithing and home security are important aspects of keeping your property and possessions safe from harm. However, it's normal to have questions about lock systems, locksmiths, and other security measures. Here are some of the most frequently asked questions about locksmithing and home security.

What services do locksmiths offer?

Locksmiths are skilled professionals who can install, repair, and replace all types of locks, including residential, commercial, and automotive. Additionally, they can help you gain access to your property if you've accidentally locked yourself out, make duplicate keys, and even install high-security locks to enhance your property's security.

How can I tell if a locksmith is legitimate?

Legitimate locksmiths hold licenses in their respective states, which are usually displayed on their websites. They should also ask to see your identification and proof of ownership before they begin working on your locks.

How can I improve my home's security?

There are several ways to improve your home's security, such as installing deadbolts on all your exterior doors, using security cameras, and upgrading to high-security locks with key control. You can also consider reinforcing your entry doors, adding lighting to your property, and using a home security system.

What should I do if I lost my house keys?

If you lose your house keys, the best course of action would be to call a professional locksmith to have your locks rekeyed or to have a new lock installed altogether. This will ensure that anyone who might have found or stolen your keys won't be able to gain access to your home.

How often should I change my locks?

It's a good idea to change your locks every five to seven years, depending on the wear and tear of your locks. Additionally, if you suspect that someone has copied your keys or you've had a break-in, it's important to change your locks immediately.
